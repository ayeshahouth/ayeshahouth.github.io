Intro

Hello, I'm Ayesha Baloch, a Data Analyst based in Bahrain. I have a passion for using data to solve business problems and provide valuable insights. I have experience in using industry standard analytics tools such as Excel, SQL, Power BI, Tableau, Python and Oracle Analytics Cloud to manage, analyze and communicate large amounts of data concisely to inform business decisions and strategies. I am motivated by learning, collaborating, and creating a positive impact. I uphold professional values of reliability, positivity, and innovation.

Work

Here are some of my projects:

1. Kickstarter Project: Identifying the success factors that impact US based projects from the film and video category from 2009 to 2012.
View Project
Tools Used: “Excel” for data analysis and visualization and PowerPoint for presentation.

2. Superstore Project: Identifying the main factors that contributed to the decline in profit margins from 2016 to 2019.
View Project
Tools Used: “PostgreSQL” for data exploration and analysis, “Excel” for data visualization, and “Canva” for presentation.

3. World Freedom of Expression Project: The impact of some political factors on the change in freedom of expression from 2005 to 2021 worldwide.
View Project
Tools Used: “Tableau” for data analysis, data visualization and story (presentation).

4. US Wind Turbines Analysis Project using Agile Scrum Methodology: To identify the optimal plant to invest in, based on the environmental conditions of the state and the maximum turbine capacity as the scrum master.
View Project
Tools Used: “Tableau” for data analysis, data visualization and story (presentation). “Trello” for agile scrum methodology.

5. US Stocks Analysis Capstone Project: This project aims to provide US stock recommendations for investors of different age ranges with relative risk preferences, and objectives by examining key historical financial ratios to evaluate companies' performance, growth, and risk and then aligning stocks to investors based on their specific situation.
View Project
Tools Used: “Python” for data extraction using the Financial Modeling Prep API, “Power BI” for data analysis and visualization and “PowerPoint” for presentation.
